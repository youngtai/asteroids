# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Friends, families, and kids who want to quickly choose and play simple browser games together. Players may be on a keyboard or gamepad, and they should be able to get from the game portal into a playable game without reading setup-heavy instructions.

## Product Purpose

This project is a lightweight game portal: a single place to browse small games, launch them on their own routes, and keep each game focused on immediate play. Success means the portal feels clear and durable enough to add more games without turning each addition into another one-off static page.

## Brand Personality

Clean arcade: crisp, high-contrast, playful, and direct. The app should feel approachable for kids and families while still respecting the fast, no-friction rhythm of arcade games.

## Anti-references

Avoid casino, gambling, or dark nightclub aesthetics. Avoid promotional landing-page patterns that put marketing ahead of play. Avoid complex dashboard conventions that make the portal feel like work instead of a quick game picker.

## Design Principles

1. Launch fast: the primary path is choosing a game and playing it.
2. Keep games self-contained: each game owns its play surface while sharing the same app shell.
3. Design for shared play: controls, routes, and labels should support friends and families passing between games.
4. Stay clean under expansion: new games should be easy to add without duplicating app-level structure.
5. Preserve directness: the interface should explain itself through clear labels and familiar controls.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain keyboard-first access and gamepad support where games provide it. Use high-contrast text and controls, avoid relying on color alone for status, keep motion purposeful, and respect reduced-motion preferences in app-level UI.
